Understanding the Challenge

Problem & Objective

The Problem

Current cancer treatment systems fail to provide personalized drug ranking that considers both the molecular profile of the cancer (the specific protein affected) and the patient's comorbid conditions. This leads to suboptimal treatment choices, increased risk of adverse drug reactions, and compromised patient outcomes. Oncologists need a comprehensive system that evaluates safety across multiple medications.

Our Objective

Develop a personalized cancer-disease drug ranking system that integrates quantum-enhanced binding affinity prediction, molecular docking, ADMET profiling, side-effect severity scoring, and drug-drug interaction analysis. The system retrieves FDA-approved therapies for comorbid conditions and selects the safest, most effective treatment pairs using multi-criteria decision analysis (TOPSIS).

Module 04

Quantum-Enhanced Binding Affinity

A hybrid quantum-classical neural network (QCNN) for predicting drug-protein binding affinity. Uses character tokenization, 128-dim embeddings, Conv1D layers, and replaces classical attention with a quantum circuit: Amplitude Encoding converts data to quantum states, Hadamard gates create superposition, and RX/RY/RZ variational gates perform learnable rotations analogous to trainable weights.

PennyLane PyTorch VQC Hybrid QCNN

Output

Module 05

Drug-Drug Interaction Prediction

A Random Forest classifier that predicts interactions between cancer drugs and comorbid disease drugs. Extracts physicochemical features (MolWt, LogP, TPSA, etc.) from both drug SMILES using RDKit, computes Tanimoto similarity between molecular fingerprints, and classifies interactions as Safe (0), Moderate (1), or Unsafe (2) using the DrugBank dataset (191,809 pairs).

Measurement

Why Quantum?

Amplitude Encoding converts classical feature vectors into quantum states, compressing information into qubit amplitudes. Hadamard gates map to exponentially large feature spaces, eliminating the need for multi-head attention. Variational gates (RX, RY, RZ) act as learnable parameters analogous to neural network weights, trained through backpropagation via PennyLane.

Architecture Comparison

The classical CNN uses Conv1D layers + Multi-Head Attention + Fully Connected layers (256-dim). The quantum model replaces attention with 3 VQCs (4 qubits each), concatenated to 12-dim, then projected through fully connected layers. This achieves 81.25% accuracy with 37ms inference time and Pearson correlation of 0.73.

Ranking Algorithm

TOPSIS Workflow

Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution — ranks alternatives based on closeness to the best possible outcome.

1

Decision Matrix

Construct matrix: Alternatives (drugs) x Criteria (affinity, torsions, ADMET, severity, DDI)

2

Normalize

Apply Euclidean normalization to each column for comparable scales

3

Weight Criteria

Multiply normalized values by criterion-specific weights

4

Ideal Solutions

Identify PIS (best value) and NIS (worst value) for each criterion

5

Calculate Distances

Compute Euclidean distance of each alternative from PIS and NIS

6

Closeness Coefficient

CC = S- / (S+ + S-). Higher CC = closer to ideal. Rank by descending CC.

Summary

Key Takeaways

Holistic Patient-Centric Ranking

Combining quantum-enhanced affinity predictions with docking, ADMET, side-effect scoring, and DDI prediction delivers a truly multidimensional evaluation of cancer-comorbidity drug pairs.

Quantum-Classical Advantage

The hybrid model uncovers subtle molecular interactions that improve binding affinity estimates beyond classical methods, strengthening overall ranking fidelity.

Clinical Translation Focus

Restricting to FDA-approved drugs and using validated datasets (KIBA, SIDER, DrugBank) ensures the system's recommendations are clinically relevant and actionable.
